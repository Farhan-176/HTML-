An Electronic Cigarette (E-cigarette) or Vape is a device that simulates tobacco smoking.
It consists of an

  • Atomizer
  • A Power source such as a battery
  • A Container such as a cartridge or tank filled with liquid
Instead of smoke, the user inhales vapor.As such, using an E-cigarette is often called "Vaping".

  1. Construction

    2. E-cigarettes have evolved over time, and the different designs are classified in generations.

    • 1st -generation e-cigarettes tend to look like traditional cigarettes and are called "cigalikes".
    • 2nd-generation devices are larger and look less like traditional cigarettes.
    • 3rd-generationdevices include mechanical mods and variable voltage devices
    • The 4th-generation includes sub-ohm tanks (meaning they have electrical resistance of less than 1 ohm) and temperature control
    There are also pod mod devices that use protonated nicotine, rather than free-base nicotine found in earlier generations,providing higher nicotine yields.

           

    E-Liquid :

    The mixture used in vapor products such as e-cigarettes is called e-liquid.E-liquid formulations vary widely.A typical e-liquid is composed of propylene glycol and glycerin (95%) and a combination of flavorings, nicotine, and other additives (5%).

  2. Use

    3. Since entering the market around 2003, e-cigarette use has risen rapidly.In 2011 there were about 7 million adult e-cigarette users globally, increasing to 68 million in 2020 compared with 1.1 billion cigarette smokers.There was a further rise to 82 million e-cigarette users in 2021.E-cigarette use is highest in China, the US, and Europe, with China having the most users.

  3. Health effects

    4. The health risks of e-cigarettes are not known for certain, but the risk of serious adverse events is thought to be low,and e-cigarettes are likely safer than combusted tobacco products.However, this does not mean that e-cigarettes are harmless. E-cigarette use is associated with increased risk of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease,asthma ,chronic bronchitis, and emphysema. Those who use e-cigarettes daily have higher risk than those who use them occasionally.According to the NASM,Laboratory tests of e-cigarette ingredients, in vitro toxicological tests, and short-term human studies suggest that e-cigarettes are likely to be far less harmful than combustible tobacco cigarettes.
